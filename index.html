He did it, GSA Chairman Turki Al-Sheikh wrote a tweet in response to Kuwait’s Minister of Commerce
Posted on November 4, 2019November 4, 2019 By Annmarie Garza

He did it, GSA Chairman Turki Al-Sheikh wrote a tweet in response to Kuwait’s Minister of Commerce

“Al-Roudan, in brief, is a mercenary under the umbrella of position and is also goes against proven facts. This mercenary will not affect the historical relations between Saudi Arabia and its sister Kuwait, and what he has said only represents him, as Modar poet said: If you want to know the souls, throw them money”

This is what the Chairman of the General Sports Authority and Advisor of the Saudi Royal Court, Turki Al-Sheikh as he made a public statement on Twitter.

This tweet responds to his dissatisfaction at Khalid Nasser Al-Roudan, Kuwait’s Minister of Commerce and Industry and Minister of State for Youth Affairs. It was tweeted after the meeting between the Minister and Emir Sheik Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani in Doha.

Al-Sheikh’s tweet came as a result of the gratitude of Kuwaiti Minister Khalid Al-Roudhan to the Emir of Qatar for his help and support in lifting last month’s FIFA suspension of the Kuwait Football Association. The delegation headed by the Ministry also thanked Qatar for its decision to move the 23rd Gulf Cup to Kuwait.

“We assert our rejection and astonishment at this insult because of its impact on the warm and distinguished fraternal relations between the two sisterly countries,” Deputy Kuwaiti Foreign Minister Khaled al-Jarallah at an Indian embassy reception.

Minister Khaled al-Jarallah has conveyed his “regrets and condemnations” to the Saudi Ambassador to Kuwait regarding the perceptions addressed to the Minister of Youth and Sport, Khalid al-Rodhan.

The tweet, which was in pretty poor taste, shows the real social media atmosphere in the countries that block Qatar, where many officials and media commentators use foul language and false information to discredit other Gulf countries. Although a number of Kuwaiti people in social media have been seen voicing their frustration at the Al-Sheikh Tweet, and they all hoped that this would soon be resolved.
